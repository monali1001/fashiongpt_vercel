How to Determine Your Body Type

To determine your body type, follow these steps:

1. Stand in front of a mirror and wear fitted clothing or undergarments to see your body's natural shape.

2. Measure your shoulders at their widest point.

3. Measure your bust at the fullest part.

4. Measure your waist at the narrowest point, typically just above the belly button.

5. Measure your hips at the widest part, typically around the hip bones.

Based on these measurements, you can identify your body type as follows:

  • Rectangle/Straight: Your shoulders, bust, and hips are approximately the same width, with little definition at the waist.
  • Triangle/Pear: Your hips are wider than your shoulders and bust, with a well-defined waist.
  • Spoon: Similar to a pear shape, but your hips have a more pronounced shelf appearance.
  • Hourglass: Your shoulders and hips are about the same width, with a well-defined waist.
  • Top hourglass: Your shoulders are slightly wider than your hips, with a well-defined waist.
  • Bottom hourglass: Your hips are slightly wider than your shoulders, with a well-defined waist.
  • Inverted triangle/Apple: Your shoulders are wider than your hips, with little definition at the waist.
  • Round/Oval: Your bust and waist are larger than your hips, with a fuller midsection.
  • Diamond: Your waist is larger than your bust and hips, with a narrower bust and hips.
  • Athletic: Your body is muscular with broader shoulders and narrower hips, often with a less defined waist.
